STARGON INVESTIGATION

Let’s call the following figures stargons. They are labeled (a, b). They are formed by joining a points on a circle according to a certain rule, b. The stargon in figure 1 is referred to as the stargon (5, 2) because there are 5 points, or vertices, and the figure is formed by joining every second point. What is the name of the stargon in figure 2?

How are these two stargons different? There are two kinds of stargons that can result from the rule and the number of points. How does the type of stargon depend on a and b? What happens when b = 1? What happens if a is even and b = a/2? Investigate! What else can you find about this?

What is the sum of the measures of the exterior angles formed at the vertices of the stargon (5, 2)? (One exterior angle at each vertex). Investigate this for other stargons. Find a general formula that gives the sum of the exterior angle measures for any stargon (a, b).

What is the sum of the interior angle measures at the vertices of the stargon (5, 2)? Investigate this for other stargons. Find a general formula that gives the sum of the interior angles for any stargon (a, b).

What result do you get when b = 1 for each of these formulas?
